## Hard-endpoint data-scale continuation gate

Commit `b59ec05018b3b1c2e1b815dbd9f3346edbf5a16e` closes a scientific loophole in the original
data-scaling protocol. Previously, the 250/500/1,000-row curve could recommend more
same-distribution data from validation mask IoU alone. That is insufficient to show that data volume
addresses the intended O4 transfer, missing-detector, low-SNR or rare-glitch failure modes. The raw
curve is therefore retained as a diagnostic and can no longer satisfy the publication ledger or
authorize scale 2,000 by itself.

only, requires exact commit/config/corpus hashes and predeclares four strata with at least 25 rows
and 25 unique glitches each: low observed-network SNR, missing detector, O3b transfer and rare
glitch family. Observed-network SNR is computed only over available detectors. Every
scale/control/seed cell must replay the same frozen subset, checkpoint and validation-selected chirp
and glitch thresholds with zero test-set refits.

The immutable binder requires the complete paired fixed-epoch and fixed-update matrix. On the latest
doubling, both controls must achieve a hard-endpoint gain of at least 0.01 with paired-bootstrap
lower confidence bound above zero, while every upper-scale seed retains at least 95% clean
performance. Only that joint result may authorize one bounded next scale. Otherwise the report
retains the negative or null outcome and classifies the limitation as clean non-inferiority,
optimization budget, domain transfer, or representation/label failure instead of blindly scaling.

bootstrap uncertainty. The distinct paper contribution is mask-preserving, detector-set-aware
glitch robustness and improved downstream posterior reliability under contamination. DINGO and
AMPLFI are compared only on shared PE coverage, bias, width, sky area, sample efficiency and latency,
never as detection-mAP baselines.
