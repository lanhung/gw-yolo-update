## Training-data conclusion

The earlier concern that training data was too small was correct, but “more rows” is
not the whole answer:

1. The physical glitch corpus has grown from the initial bank to 3,016 independent
   numeric network samples and now supports a real source-safe split.
2. The current clean injection bank, not the glitch count, is the immediate overlap
   bottleneck. It supports only 1,170 strict H1+L1 train pairs.
3. Scaling beyond 1,000 should not start by schedule. First compare 250/500/1,000 under
   both fixed-epoch and fixed-update controls on the frozen hard O4a endpoint.
4. If the endpoint improves materially, the next data investment should be a
   variable-IFO H1/L1/V1 injection bank and later-run/OOD glitch coverage, not duplicated
   rendered images or mosaic/mixup.
5. If the endpoint saturates, the limitation is more likely representation, calibration
   or domain transfer; increasing nominal image count would not create a phase change.

## Critical path and forecast

The earliest credible compute-only path to a 10/10 validation freeze is approximately
4–8 days, assuming the one-seed gate passes and the remote service remains available.
The human-mask item has no honest automatic ETA because two additional genuine people
must annotate; with reviewers available, the work itself is typically one day.

After validation 10/10, the one-time locked suite is expected to take roughly 1–3 weeks.
The uncertainty is dominated by sequential O4b source transfer and post-access streaming,
not GPU inference. A paper-grade analysis and manuscript would then require another
2–4 weeks for five-seed tables, uncertainty figures, ablations, negative-result retention
and internal review. A realistic end-to-end forecast is therefore **4–8 weeks**, not an
unqualified promise of a target accuracy.

The result target remains unchanged: demonstrate at common FAR that explicit masks and
network coherence recover sensitivity or downstream posterior reliability under
nonstationary/overlapping artifacts, while preserving clean-data non-inferiority.
DINGO and AMPLFI remain downstream PE backends; the defensible comparison is their paired
raw/contaminated/mask-conditioned change under identical events, not detection mAP.
