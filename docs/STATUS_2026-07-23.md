## Executive status

The project is approximately **37% complete against the publication objective**. This estimate
separates implementation from evidence: reproducible engineering is approximately **99.2%**, the
validation-to-locked pre-access protocol is approximately **99.7%**, but accepted paper evidence is
only approximately **15%**. The O4b/GWTC-5 locked-final ledger remains **0/9 by design**, because the
validation ledger is not yet 10/10 and the access log is still absent.

No claim of superiority to AMPLFI or DINGO is currently supported. The frozen comparison is
within-backend and paired across clean, contaminated and mask-conditioned inputs. The intended
distinct advantage remains mask-aware deglitching and search/PE robustness under overlapping
artifacts, not clean-data detection mAP or an absolute posterior-backend ranking.

## Variable-detector locked search closure

Commit `b07787a` closes a previously hidden locked-evaluation failure: the suite declared H1+L1,
H1+V1, L1+V1 and H1+L1+V1, while the old reducer could only construct H1/L1 pairs and did not
create the four predeclared final search inputs. Injection ranking now evaluates every eligible
detector subset, applies every pairwise light-travel limit plus the frozen empirical timing
allowance, retains explicit missing-IFO semantics and prefers a larger coherent network only when
the ranking score ties.

The pre-access streaming plan now freezes a score-blind independent-offset schedule with H1 at
zero, L1 shifted forward and V1 shifted backward. Raw and mask arms replay the same schedule,
physical denominator and injection trigger set. Detector duty cycle is evaluated at each shifted
source window; all eligible subset channels are clustered jointly, so a single event cannot be
counted four times. The reduction fails closed unless every detector subset and source family is
covered and the background, injection, OOD and paired-PE minima all pass.

A read-only replay against the real 4,860-row O4b availability manifest selected 65 effective
offsets through slide index 65. It predicts 34.6749654 equivalent years, just above the
predeclared 1.5× safety target of 34.5387764 years. The schedule contains 177,421 H1+L1,
183,564 H1+V1, 186,970 L1+V1 and 140,401 H1+L1+V1 eligible shifted block instances. This is
pre-access capacity evidence only: the final reducer still requires at least 23.02585093 years
after DQ attrition and will not replace rejected blocks.
